ForestFires in Kazakhstan

Forest fires in Kazakhstan pose a significant threat to the country's biodiversity and natural resources, with thousands of hectares of forestland being destroyed annually. 

The government and local communities are actively implementing measures for early detection, rapid response, and sustainable forest management to mitigate the devastating impacts of these fires.

Fire Safety: 
Your Protection and Your Responsibility

Every year, thousands of lives and tens of thousands of property are lost due to fires. 
But knowing about fire safety rules and accepting responsibility can save lives and save valuable property.

Fires do not only destroy buildings and property, but also cause irreparable damage to families, communities and the environment. Understanding the risks and taking action to prevent fires is the key to safety and well-being.

Social factor

In 2019, the Amazon rainforest experienced the highest number of wildfires in a decade, with over 906,000 fire alerts reported.

The devastating wildfires in Australia during the 2019-2020 bushfire season resulted in the loss of 34 human lives and caused the displacement of thousands of people.

Environmental damage

According to the Global Forest Resources Assessment 2020, wildfires affect an estimated 350 million hectares of forests worldwide annually.

According to the World Wildlife Fund (WWF), more than 50% of the world's tropical forests have been lost due to fires, logging, and other human activities.

Economic factor

According to a report by the European Parliament, the economic cost of forest fires in Europe is estimated to be around €3 billion per year.

The Australian bushfires in 2019-2020 resulted in an estimated economic loss of AUD 103 billion (USD 72.7 billion) and impacted various sectors, including tourism, agriculture, and infrastructure.

Fire safety

01
Fire safety begins with prevention:

 Ensure the presence of functioning smoke detectors, maintain clear evacuation routes, and regularly inspect and maintain electrical systems and appliances to minimize the risk of fire incidents.

02
Effective fire safety also involves preparedness: 

Develop and practice a fire escape plan, educate household members or employees about fire safety procedures, and keep fire extinguishers readily accessible in key locations.

03
In the event of a fire, swift action is crucial: 

Alert emergency services immediately, evacuate the premises following established protocols, and never re-enter a burning building. Remember, prioritizing safety and minimizing potential hazards is paramount in fire prevention and response.
